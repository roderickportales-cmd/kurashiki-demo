Why Visit Kurashiki?

Historic Bikan District

Walk along canals lined with white-walled storehouses and willow trees from Japan’s Edo period.

Ohara Museum of Art

Japan’s first museum of Western art, home to masterpieces by Monet, El Greco, and Renoir.

Local Crafts & Textiles

Discover Kurashiki’s artisan denim, paper goods, and handmade souvenirs reflecting local heritage.

Things to Experience

Canal Boat Ride

Glide along Kurashiki’s scenic canals in a traditional wooden boat. It’s a relaxing way to take in willow-lined streets and historic architecture.

Kurashiki Ivy Square

Once a textile mill, this red-brick complex is now a hub for shopping, dining, and museums. Its ivy-covered walls make it one of the city’s most iconic sights.

Traditional Street Food

Sample local specialties like kibidango (sweet dumplings) and freshly grilled skewers. Street stalls and small eateries offer flavors you won’t forget.

Denim Workshops

Kurashiki is known as the birthplace of Japanese denim. Visit workshops where artisans handcraft high-quality jeans and accessories.

Local Festivals

Experience vibrant seasonal festivals with traditional music, parades, and performances. They offer a lively glimpse into Kurashiki’s community spirit.

Cycling Trails

Explore Kurashiki and its surrounding countryside on two wheels. The bike-friendly paths lead through rice fields, riversides, and charming villages.

Museum Street

This cultural street is lined with art museums and galleries. It’s the perfect stop for those who want to blend history with creativity.

Handmade Souvenirs

Browse shops filled with local crafts, pottery, and paper goods. Each handmade item carries a piece of Kurashiki’s tradition back home with you.

Plan Your Trip to Kurashiki
